Every year on 9th September: World EV Day for awareness

To facilitate smooth growth in this domain to launch variable percentage of vehicle models in this domain policies and schemes launched by the Indian government are:

Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of (Hybrid &) Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) Scheme was launced in 2015. From 2019, phase-II of this scheme has been implemented with an outlay of INR 10,000 Cr for a period of 5 years. Out of total budgetary support, about 86% of the fund has been allocated to create demand for EVs in the country

PLI Scheme for National Programme on Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) Battery Storage was launched in 2021 to build and enhance India's manufacturing capabilities in ACC domain with an outlay of INR 18,100 Cr for seven years. The second phase is yet to be launced.

Phased Manufacturing Programme (PMP)

Apart from schemes several other initiativess are taken like:
GST on EVs and charging stations reduced from 12% to 5%.
Interest Subvention: Discount offered on the interest rate while availing loan

The Union Budget 2023-24 extended the customs duty exemption to the import of capital goods and machinery required for the manufacture of lithium-ion cells for batteries used in electric vehicles.

Both commercial as well as private battery-operated vehicles are given green licencse plates.

To expand and strengthen the Public Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure across the nation, the Ministry of Power issued certain guildelines under which private players have announced of setting upto 22k EV charging stations in metro cities and on National Highways.













Future: (Caraousel)
It is estimated that the country’s EV market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 49% between 2022 and 2030 and that the annual sales of EVs in 2030 may cross one crore units, leading to the creation of five crore direct and indirect jobs by 2030.

Major players in the industry, including Maruti Suzuki, Hyundai, Mahindra & Mahindra, Tata Motors, as well as luxury carmakers like Mercedes-Benz and Audi, are all gearing up to roll out an array of EV models over the next few years.

Like as: M&M is set to introduce five new BEVs from January 2025 and is aiming to cover 20-30% of it's portfolio to be electric by 2027.

The adoption of EVs will not only positively impact the country’s economy but also help in achieving India’s target of net zero emissions by 2070.India is to become a global giant in EV domain by 2030.
